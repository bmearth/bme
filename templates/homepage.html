Welcome to Burning Man Earth

Mapping the Cultural Genome

Would you like to fly over Black Rock City, zoom to your favorite theme camp, zip out to deep-playa art installations, and connect with your fellow participants, year round? Welcome to Burning Man Earth: a 3D virtual representation of Black Rock City. It's available year round, so you can learn what was done, how, and connect with who made it happen. Burning Man Earth is a mapping of the Burning Man cultural genome.

Burning Man participants are encouraged to populate this virtual city just as we do the real-world BRC. Just send us a "placemark" (which might contain your location, photograph, information, etc.), and/or an image of your camp or art installation, and we'll include it in Burning Man Earth. Learn more below.

Note: The following files are simply the beginning. Additional files, extending the scope, will be made available on this site, and automatically through the Network Link.

Get Started: Follow These Easy Steps

  1. Download and install the latest version of Google Earth.
  2. Download and open the 2008 BRC data file This network link file fill will automatically get you the latest data as we update it.
  3. Finally, add images of your theme camp or art installation (see below).

Send Us Your Theme Camp or Art Installation Placemark!

  1. Find your camp or art installation location in Burning Man Earth.
  2. Click "Add Placemark".
  3. Provide your theme camp or installation name, and any details you'd like.
  4. Then, right-click on your new placemark in the "Places" window, and send it to bmanearth (at) burningman (dot) com.
  5. Optionally (and ideally), attach to the email a photograph or a 3D image of your camp using SketchUp. Please keep the file size (number of polygons) to a minimum!
  6. You can also download 3D models of common camp elements so you can build your virtual camp and send that to us.
